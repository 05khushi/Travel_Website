Rajasthan is a state that truly captures the essence of India's royal heritage. The majestic forts and palaces in Jaipur, Jodhpur, and Udaipur are breathtaking, with each one telling a unique story of the past. I was particularly impressed by the grandeur of the Amber Fort and the stunning architecture of the City Palace in Udaipur. The vibrant markets filled with colorful textiles and traditional handicrafts are a shopper's paradise. My only downside was the heat during the summer months, so I would recommend visiting in the cooler season. Overall, Rajasthan offers a regal experience like no other.

Rishikesh is a haven for those seeking spiritual solace and adventure. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas along the Ganges River, the town is known for its ashrams, yoga retreats, and the iconic Laxman Jhula bridge. I participated in a week-long yoga retreat that was transformative, both physically and mentally. The evening Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat is a soul-stirring experience that shouldn't be missed. For adventure enthusiasts, Rishikesh offers thrilling white-water rafting experiences. The serene environment, combined with the spiritual energy, makes Rishikesh a must-visit destination in India.
